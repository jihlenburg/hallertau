**Zusammenfassung:** For 0.5–2 ha hop fields on a 7 m trellis, no free optical satellite is truly field-precise: Sentinel-2's 10 m pixels yield only ~5 (0.5 ha) to ~50 (2 ha) cells per field, and the vertical bine canopy over wide bare alleys mixes soil/shadow/canopy so heavily that S2 is honestly a regional-screening / coarse-anomaly tool, not a teilflächengenau one — the same conclusion vineyard/orchard literature reaches (77–84% mapping accuracy, area errors 2.7–13.2%). The cutting-edge access landscape splits into "bring data to compute" platforms (Google Earth Engine, openEO/CDSE, Microsoft Planetary Computer) and "data lake + STAC + COG" (AWS Open Data via Element84 Earth Search, CDSE STAC, Planet). For an EU small-farm SaaS the clear best-in-class default is the Copernicus Data Space Ecosystem: 10,000 free openEO credits/month, a STAC API, and — crucially — the Sentinel Hub Statistical API that returns server-side per-field zonal-statistics time series with cloud masking built in, so almost no raster ever touches your infrastructure. Recommended pipeline: ESA L2A (BOA, already atmospherically corrected) → Cloud Score+/s2cloudless masking → Whittaker/Savitzky-Golay smoothing → per-field zonal stats → cache in PostGIS, with a Python worker only for the rare heavy raster job. GEE/Planet are prototyping or precision add-ons, not the production backbone for a cost-sensitive German hop SaaS.

**Feldskalen-Verdikt:** Field-precise: NO for any free optical satellite. A 0.5 ha hop field holds only ~5 whole Sentinel-2 10 m pixels (often ≤2–3 pure interior pixels); 1 ha ≈ 10–20; 2 ha ≈ 30–50. Worse, the ~7 m vertical trellis over wide bare alleys means each nadir pixel mixes canopy + soil + structural shadow, systematically diluting and confounding NDVI in a way flat crops don't suffer. So Sentinel-2 is honestly REGIONAL-SCREENING / per-field relative-trend & anomaly detection, not teilflächengenau — matching vineyard-analogue results (77–84% mapping accuracy, 2.7–13.2% area error) and the project's own 10m-on-7m-Gerüst guardrail. Planet 3 m (commercial, daily) reaches "field-level trend" but still mixes on the trellis; only UAV/drone multispectral is genuinely field-precise. Product design implication: present the Feld-Check as a trend/anomaly status card (Ampel), never as a within-field prescription map, and reserve "field-precise" claims for an optional UAV/Planet premium tier.

**Infrastruktur-Implikationen:** Ingest: prefer "data-to-compute" over a local archive — query STAC (CDSE, Earth Search v1, MS PC) and call CDSE Sentinel Hub Statistical API for server-side per-field zonal stats with cloud masking, so almost no raster touches your servers. Compute: a Node/TS worker (BullMQ or pg-boss) on the existing doldenblick-01 box handles the common path entirely in HTTP+JSON (token-cached OAuth, Whittaker/SG smoothing in JS), with an optional stateless Python/FastAPI microservice (stackstac/rasterio over Earth Search COGs) only for heavy custom raster jobs or Planet/UAV. Storage: PostGIS holds field polygons + cached NDVI/anomaly time series + derived status; S3/MinIO caches occasional COG chips and rendered MapLibre tiles — do NOT mirror the archive. Scheduler: nightly per-field cron fits the abendliches-Briefing model and stays inside CDSE's free 10,000 credits/month at Huber scale (~€0/mo). Source-agnostic STAC adapter gives failover across CDSE/AWS/MS. Skip building atmospheric correction (use ESA L2A BOA) and skip GEE on the critical path (the $2,000/mo flat fee is uneconomic at small-farm scale).

## The crux first: can a sensor resolve a hop field? (be honest)

Hop fields in the Hallertau are 0.5–2 ha, planted on a ~7 m vertical trellis (Gerüst) with wide (~3 m) alleys. Two facts dominate everything below:

- **Pixel count is tiny.** A 0.5 ha field (~50 × 100 m) holds only ~5 *whole* 10 m Sentinel-2 pixels; edge/mixed pixels make the count of *pure* interior pixels often ≤ 2–3. A 1 ha field ≈ 10–20 usable interior pixels; 2 ha ≈ 30–50. Per-field zonal statistics are therefore averages over a handful of cells, with high variance and strong edge contamination from neighbouring fields, tracks and hedgerows.
- **The canopy is vertical over bare soil.** Unlike a flat closed crop, a hop "row" is bines climbing wires with wide bare/grassed alleys between. A nadir 10 m pixel integrates canopy + alley soil + structural shadow, so NDVI is systematically *diluted* and seasonally confounded by row orientation, sun angle and trellis shadow. This is worse than the vineyard/orchard analogue, which already shows trouble.

**Analogue evidence (flag: analogue, not hops).** Global vineyard mapping with Sentinel-2 + random forest reaches **77–84% overall accuracy**, with area estimates off by **2.7–13.2%**, worst in fragmented landscapes, with explicit spectral confusion between vineyards and shrub/orchard cover ([vineyard mapping study via search](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/10106049.2024.2361733)). Vendors are blunt: 10 m "is good enough to collect measurements over small fields" but "field delineation is not well suited to smallholder farms due to the 10 m resolution" ([Earth Blox Sentinel-2 review](https://www.earthblox.io/resources/earth-blox-dataset-review-sentinel-2)).

**Hops-specific literature is thin.** The one directly relevant study (Czech, Žatec cultivars) used a **UAV (eBee X, Sequoia multispectral) plus Sentinel-2** and found "significant differences in NDVI among gardens and cultivars," confirming both work — but it leans on UAV for the fine detail and does not claim per-plant precision from S2 ([ISHS Acta Hort. 1328/13](https://ishs.org/ishs-article/1328_13/)). 

**Verdict for product framing:** Sentinel-2 (free) = **regional screening / relative anomaly & phenology trend per field**, consistent with the CLAUDE.md guardrail ("Satellit = regionales Screening … 10-m-Pixel auf 7-m-Gerüst"). Planet 3 m (commercial) improves linear resolution ~3× and gives daily revisit, edging toward "field-level trend" but still mixing on the 7 m trellis. **Only UAV/drone multispectral is truly teilflächengenau.** Design the "Feld-Check" as a *trend/anomaly* card, not a prescription map.

## Platform landscape (access models)

Two paradigms:

**A. "Bring compute to the data" (managed, server-side processing):**
- **Google Earth Engine (GEE)** — richest catalog + algorithms (incl. Cloud Score+, which is GEE-native). Commercial: **$2,000/month flat** (incl. $200 batch + $40 online credits) plus **EECU-hours at $0.40 → $0.28 → $0.16** tiered. A single global S2 composite extrapolates to **$40k–$160k/yr in compute alone**; a custom pipeline is ~$15k–$35k/yr and "wins within 2–3 years" ([Economics of Earth Engine](https://christopherren.substack.com/p/the-economics-of-earth-engine), [GEE pricing](https://cloud.google.com/earth-engine/pricing)). Great for prototyping; the flat fee is poison for a tiny SaaS. US/Google-hosted (EU-sovereignty friction).
- **Copernicus Data Space Ecosystem (CDSE)** — EU/ESA, hosted on CloudFerro. Offers **STAC API**, **openEO** (standardised, scales on the cloud beside the data), and the **Sentinel Hub APIs** (Process, **Statistical**, Catalog). Free quota: **10,000 openEO credits/month** (20,000 for Copernicus Services users); a 10 ha @ 10 m query "costs only a few cents"; synchronous request = 6 credits, batch job startup = 2 credits ([openEO credit usage](https://documentation.dataspace.copernicus.eu/APIs/openEO/credit_usage.html), [10,000 credits guide](https://dataspace.copernicus.eu/news/2024-9-23-discover-how-make-most-your-10000-openeo-credits)). March 2026 billing update cut credit consumption ~**25%** and moved openEO onto the new STAC backend ([billing update](https://dataspace.copernicus.eu/news/2026-3-2-platform-wide-updates-openeo-credit-billing)).
- **Microsoft Planetary Computer** — Data + STAC API remain free and current (S2 L2A as COG, 2016–present; HLS added), **but the interactive Hub was retired 6 June 2024**; the commercial path is **Planetary Computer Pro** (private data, Azure, pricing tbd) ([Hub retirement #347](https://github.com/microsoft/PlanetaryComputer/discussions/347), [PC S2 L2A](https://planetarycomputer.microsoft.com/dataset/sentinel-2-l2a)). Usable as a free STAC+COG source from your own code; not a managed compute backbone anymore.

**B. "Data lake + STAC + COG" (you process):**
- **AWS Open Data — Element84 Earth Search v1** (`earth-search.aws.element84.com/v1`): free **Sentinel-2 L2A Cloud-Optimized GeoTIFFs** with STAC, the de-facto open standard for `stackstac`/`odc-stac`/`rasterio` workflows; you pay only AWS compute + egress ([Earth Search v1](https://element84.com/geospatial/introducing-earth-search-v1-new-datasets-now-available/), [S2 L2A COGs registry](https://registry.opendata.aws/sentinel-2-l2a-cogs/)).
- **Planet** — commercial PlanetScope/SuperDove **3 m, 8-band, daily**; subscription priced by AoI area (sales-quoted, not public). The precision/revisit upgrade if/when a paying tier is justified ([Planet pricing](https://www.planet.com/pricing/), [PlanetScope docs](https://docs.planet.com/data/imagery/planetscope/)). Also resold through Sentinel Hub as a third-party layer (per-ha charges).

## The processing pipeline (what's state of the art in 2024–2026)

1. **Catalog / STAC** — query by AoI + date + cloud%. CDSE STAC API or Earth Search v1. Both OGC-STAC compliant.
2. **Atmospheric correction** — *don't build it*: use **L2A (bottom-of-atmosphere)**; ESA's Sen2Cor already applied it. CDSE, PC and Earth Search all serve L2A.
3. **Cloud & shadow masking** — current best practice is **layered, not single-mask**: use **Cloud Score+** (best F1 ≈ **0.85** vs s2cloudless 0.76; masks shadow-affected usability) as the primary usability score, **s2cloudless** as a tunable cloud-probability cross-check, **SCL** for semantic shadow/snow/water, then temporal QA to catch the rest. No universal winner; rankings shift by scene ([ClearSKY mask comparison](https://clearsky.vision/knowledge/best-sentinel-2-cloud-mask-scl-vs-s2cloudless-vs-fmask)). Note Cloud Score+ is easiest inside GEE; outside it, s2cloudless + SCL is the pragmatic free combo. **Fmask 4.7** is what HLS uses internally ([HLS algorithms](https://hls.gsfc.nasa.gov/algorithms/)).
4. **BRDF / harmonization** — at single-field zonal-mean scale BRDF effects are second-order; if you want Landsat+Sentinel fusion for denser time series, use **HLS v2.0** (NASA, 30 m, harmonized, 2–3 day combined revisit, now incl. Sentinel-2C) rather than rolling your own — but note 30 m is *coarser* than native S2 10 m, so for tiny hop fields prefer native S2 10 m and accept sparser cadence ([HLS data products](https://hls.gsfc.nasa.gov/data-products/)).
5. **Gap-filling / smoothing** — irregular, cloud-gapped per-field NDVI series → **Whittaker smoother** (preferred: handles irregular spacing, single λ parameter, robust) or **Savitzky-Golay**. Run on the zonal-mean series, not per-pixel, given how few pixels you have.
6. **Per-field zonal statistics** — the product's core number. **Best-in-class shortcut: CDSE Sentinel Hub Statistical API** computes mean/stdev/percentile NDVI (or any index) *per polygon, per date, server-side, with cloud masking applied* and returns compact JSON — you never download a raster. This is the single biggest infrastructure win for a small SaaS.
7. **Serving** — store per-field time series + derived status in PostGIS; serve GeoJSON/MVT tiles to MapLibre; render the "Feld-Check" trend card client-side.

## Cost models (concrete)

- **CDSE Sentinel Hub Statistical API / openEO**: free 10k credits/mo. A per-field zonal-stats request over ~1–2 ha is *well under* the "few cents per 10 ha" figure; even 6 fields × daily checks × a season fits the free tier comfortably. **Effective cost for Huber-scale (6 fields/18.4 ha): ~€0/month.**
- **AWS self-process (Earth Search COGs)**: data free; cost = worker compute + egress. A nightly Lambda/Fargate job reading a few COG windows for 6 fields is cents/day; the real cost is engineering + ops.
- **GEE**: $2,000/mo flat kills it at this scale unless you're already an EE shop.
- **Planet**: commercial, AoI-priced (quote required) — reserve for a paid "Premium/UAV-grade" tier, not the base product.

## Concrete recommendation for DoldenBlick (TS/Node + PostGIS + object storage + worker)

**Primary backbone: Copernicus Data Space Ecosystem.** EU-hosted (data-sovereignty fit for Bavarian farms), free at this scale, and the Statistical API removes 90% of the raster pipeline. What runs where:

- **Node/TypeScript API (existing Fastify on doldenblick-01):** owns field geometries in **PostGIS**, exposes `/api/fields/:id/feldcheck`, returns cached NDVI/anomaly series + status. No raster work here.
- **Worker/scheduler (BullMQ or pg-boss on Postgres):** nightly per-field job that (a) calls **CDSE Sentinel Hub Statistical API** with the field polygon → gets cloud-masked zonal NDVI per available date, (b) runs **Whittaker smoothing** + simple z-score anomaly vs the field's own history, (c) upserts results into PostGIS. CDSE OAuth token cached. This is pure HTTP+JSON — fits Node directly, no Python needed for the common path.
- **Optional Python microservice (FastAPI, only if needed):** for heavier custom raster work (e.g., sub-pixel unmixing experiments, Planet 3 m, or model inference) read **Earth Search v1 COGs** with `stackstac`/`rasterio`. Invoked by the same worker; keep it stateless and behind the queue.
- **Object storage (S3/MinIO):** cache occasional COG chips / quicklook PNGs and rendered map tiles; do **not** mirror the archive — STAC + on-demand windowed COG reads make a local mirror unnecessary.
- **openEO (CDSE) for batch:** reserve for periodic regional/area-wide products (e.g., a Hallertau screening mosaic) where its server-side scaling beats per-field calls.

**Fallback / second source:** Earth Search v1 (AWS) for resilience if CDSE is degraded, and Microsoft PC STAC as a third free catalog. All three are STAC-compatible, so the worker can be source-agnostic behind a thin adapter.

**Do NOT** put GEE on the critical path (flat fee), and **do not** build atmospheric correction or a tile archive (use L2A + STAC/COG). Treat Planet/UAV as a future paid precision tier, clearly labelled in-UI as the only field-precise option.

# Vollständigkeits-Kritik — Satelliten-Recherche (2026-06-28)

> Adversarielle Schluss-Kritik des Research-Schwarms. Vor dem Bau bzw. vor Marketing-Aussagen abarbeiten.

**Verdikt:** The synthesis is a sound and unusually honest foundation to start building, and its central, repeatedly-cross-validated thesis — no free satellite is teilflaechengenau on a 0.5-2 ha hop trellis, so satellite = regional/field-mean screening, UAV = field-precise, and disease must defer to LfL/ISIP — is correct, research-grounded, and the right thing to harden into the UI and the API contract. The recommended layered free-backbone-first architecture (Sentinel-2 + S1 + HLS via CDSE STAC/Statistical API, ESA L2A BOA, thermal into the existing FAO-56 service, commercial/UAV behind feature-flags) is a defensible MVP and matches the 'Infrastruktur vor Nachfrage' premise. But it is not yet safe to treat the economic and accuracy numbers as load-bearing: the 'EUR0/month' CDSE claim ignores that CDSE flips to commercial terms at SaaS scale, the GEE '$2,000/mo' figure is stale (pricing is now negotiated), and essentially every accuracy figure (fusion R~0.8, PlanetScope R2~0.9, NDRE-N, even the hop yield R2~0.61) comes from flat-crop or single-study analogues never tested on the trellis geometry that is the whole crux. The biggest substantive holes are not sensors-on-paper but decision-relevance: effective cloud-free revisit in cloudy Bavaria is never quantified, the only validated signal is late-season/retrospective while the product is forward-looking, hyperspectral (EnMAP — German and directly relevant) is omitted, and there is no plan to validate DoldenBlick's own anomaly flags against Hallertau ground-truth. Recommendation: proceed to build the free backbone and UAV-upload path now, but gate any farmer-facing accuracy or cost claim behind (1) a local clear-sky-frequency + real-parcel-geometry backtest on actual iBALIS hop polygons, (2) re-derived CDSE/commercial cost math at multi-farm scale, and (3) an explicit ground-truth/validation loop before 'anomaly triage' is presented as more than an unverified heuristic."}

## Lücken (nicht abgedeckte Themen)
- **Hyperspectral satellites (EnMAP, PRISMA, CHIME)** — The synthesis treats hyperspectral as a UAV-only modality (Headwall/Verticillium) and omits the German DLR EnMAP (30 m, 242 bands 420-2450 nm, ~4-day tasking, operational since 2022), Italian PRISMA, and the upcoming Copernicus CHIME (~2029). EnMAP is the single most policy-relevant satellite for a German hop product and 2026 literature already fuses EnMAP/PRISMA with Sentinel-2 for canopy nitrogen. Even if 30 m is too coarse for a 0.5-2 ha trellis (and that limit should be stated), failing to assess/dismiss it on the record is a completeness hole.
- **Satellite-derived phenology as a first-class product (HR-VPP, SOS/EOS, GDD cross-check)** — Phenology is mentioned only loosely as a Sentinel-2 by-product. There is no engagement with the free Copernicus HR-VPP (High Resolution Vegetation Phenology & Productivity, 10 m) product, with formal start-/end-of-season metric extraction, or with cross-checking satellite green-up against a hop growing-degree-day (GDD) model and the LfL phenology calendar. Phenology timing is plausibly the most defensible field-AVERAGE thing satellite can deliver for hops, yet it is under-developed.
- **Effective (cloud-free) revisit in the Hallertau, not nominal revisit** — Every cadence figure (S2 ~5 d, HLS ~1.4 d, S1 ~6-12 d) is the orbital nominal. Bavaria has heavy summer convective cloud; the real clear-observation interval in the hop growing season (May-Aug) is frequently 10-20+ days. This directly undercuts the 'always-on screening backbone' value proposition and the 'evening briefing' cadence, and it is never quantified or stress-tested with a local clear-sky-frequency backtest.
- **In-season decision value vs end-of-season retrospection** — The one validated hop signal (yield R2~0.61) is explicitly BEST LATE-SEASON — i.e. after spray/irrigation decisions are made. The product is a forward-looking 'abendliches Briefing'. The synthesis never resolves the tension that the strongest evidence is retrospective/yield-estimation, while the product needs actionable in-season anomaly signal whose accuracy is unquantified. This is a value-proposition gap, not just a sensor gap.
- **Topographic / illumination correction over rolling Hallertau terrain** — The Hallertau is hilly; slope and aspect modulate reflectance and interact with the vertical trellis and row-orientation/BRDF effects the synthesis already worries about. No topographic normalization (using the DGM1 the project already references) is specified, so per-field trend comparisons across differently-oriented slopes may be biased.
- **Cross-sensor / processing-baseline harmonization for trend integrity** — The plan leans on multi-year per-field index TIME SERIES for anomaly detection, but does not address known time-series traps: the Sentinel-2 processing-baseline 04.00 (Jan 2022) BOA radiometric offset, S2A/S2B/S2C inter-calibration, and L2A reprocessing campaigns. Without explicit harmonization these create artificial 'anomalies' that would trigger false go-scout alerts.
- **Thermal sharpening to reach field scale** — ECOSTRESS (~70 m) and Landsat TIRS (100 m) are proposed as ET/water-stress cross-checks for FAO-56, but at 70-100 m a 1 ha hop field is a fraction of one pixel. The synthesis does not mention thermal sharpening/disaggregation (TsHARP/DisTrad, data-mining sharpener, or the upcoming Copernicus LSTM / NASA SBG missions), so the practical usability of space thermal at this field size is overstated and unexamined.
- **Licensing, data sovereignty and privacy of commercial/derived imagery** — Planet/Airbus/Maxar are proposed as adapters, but redistribution rights, per-seat vs per-area license terms, whether derived per-field statistics may be shown to farmers/retained, and EU/GDPR + farmer-data-sovereignty considerations for field-level geodata are not addressed. Also unaddressed: iBALIS/InVeKoS geometry usage terms and whether ISIP/LfL Peronospora data may be ingested and redistributed in a commercial SaaS.
- **In-field / proximal ground sensing as the cheap field-precise complement** — The stack jumps from satellite screening straight to UAV for 'teilflaechengenau', skipping the cheapest and most operational field-precise layer: in-field IoT (soil-moisture probes, sap-flow, leaf-wetness sensors feeding Peronospora risk) and tractor-mounted/handheld spectrometers. For a real farm these are often more practical than repeated UAV flights and would also serve as ground-truth/calibration for the satellite indices.
- **Validation / backtest / ground-truth strategy for DoldenBlick itself** — The synthesis cites others' accuracy numbers but proposes no plan to validate DoldenBlick's OWN per-field indices/anomaly flags against Hallertau ground data (LfL plots, grower yield records, scouting outcomes). Without a calibration/backtest loop, 'anomaly triage' is an untested heuristic and false-positive/negative rates are unknown.
- **Concrete commercial cost model (PlanetScope/VHR € per ha, minimum orders)** — Claims like 'tasking economics poor for mini-Schlaege' and PlanetScope as a 'paid bridge' carry no actual numbers: Planet AOI minimums, € per km2/ha, VHR tasking minimum scene sizes/prices, and how those compare to a UAV flight day. The build/no-build economics of the commercial tiers are asserted, not computed.
- **Episodic-event detection (frost, hail, storm/lodging damage)** — Hops are vulnerable to hail and storm damage on the trellis; rapid-response imaging (S1 SAR for all-weather, VHR tasking) for damage assessment/insurance is a plausible high-value use case entirely absent from the synthesis.

## Unbelegte Behauptungen (vor Verlass: Quelle oder Backtest)
- '~EUR0/month at Huber scale' via CDSE free tier: CDSE documentation explicitly states that large-scale download/processing triggers COMMERCIAL conditions, and the 10,000 free openEO credits are a fair-use general-user quota. A multi-farm commercial SaaS doing nightly per-field reductions across a season may breach the free tier; the cost figure must be re-derived for realistic multi-tenant scale and checked against CDSE's commercial-use terms, not just credit arithmetic for one farm.
- GEE '$2,000/month flat fee': Google no longer publishes flat commercial pricing — it is now usage-based (compute units + storage) plus a negotiated monthly platform fee, quoted case-by-case. The specific $2,000 figure is stale and should not be cited as fact.
- Pixel-count geometry (0.5 ha = ~25 S2 pixels; 20 m red-edge/SWIR 'collapses to ~1 reliable pixel'; 'many elongated parcels effectively zero'): the arithmetic is plausible but the claim about real parcels needs a concrete backtest against actual Hallertau iBALIS/InVeKoS hop-field polygons (their true area distribution AND elongation), not an idealized square.
- Fused Sentinel-2+PlanetScope NDVI 'R~0.8 / RMSE<0.16': derived from flat winter-wheat studies; transfer to a 7 m vertical trellis with 3 m bare alleys is unverified and should be flagged as analogue-only until tested on hop geometry.
- PlanetScope-to-Sentinel-2 consistency 'R2~0.89-0.98': winter-wheat analogue; cross-CubeSat radiometric drift on small, high-contrast trellis fields is a known risk and unverified for hops.
- Cornell grapevine downy-mildew 'RF 0.85-0.92': this is HIGH-vs-LOW incidence binary classification of ESTABLISHED disease in grapevine, not severity quantification and not hop downy mildew; the headline accuracy must not be read as transferable detection skill for P. humuli.
- Hop UAV yield 'R2~0.61' and alpha-acid 'ceiling ~18%': a single research group (CZU Prague / Zatec), 4 seasons, plot/field scale — needs independent replication and a Hallertau backtest before being treated as a general capability ceiling; 'best late-season' caveat is decision-relevant.
- NDRE foliar-N 'r~0.89, R2~0.79': from maize/soybean analogues; unverified on hop canopy and at Sentinel-2's 20 m red-edge over small fields.
- LfL Peronospora thresholds '~30/50 zoosporangia' and ISIP integration: exact operational thresholds, the primary-vs-secondary-infection logic, and crucially whether LfL/ISIP data are accessible via an API/licence that permits ingestion into a commercial product are asserted but not confirmed.
- ECOSTRESS+Landsat 'sub-field ET interoperability demonstrated': demonstrated in which crop/field-size context? Transferability to a ~1 ha trellis without thermal sharpening is unverified and likely overstated.
- Sensor-status claims as of 2026 ('S2C operational 2024', 'S1C restoring ~6 d revisit', HLS '~1.4 day'): these constellation states should be reverified against current ESA/NASA status before being designed around.
- 'Almost no raster touches your infrastructure / stays free' via Sentinel Hub Statistical API: the quota and cost math should be recomputed for nightly per-field statistics across MANY farms over a full season (worst case: cloudy reprocessing retries), not the single-Huber-farm best case.

## Fehlende Modalitäten / Sensoren
- EnMAP (German DLR hyperspectral, 30 m, 242 bands, ~4-day tasking) — must at least be assessed and dismissed on the record
- PRISMA (Italian hyperspectral, 30 m) and CHIME (Copernicus hyperspectral, ~2029) — future-roadmap relevance
- Copernicus HR-VPP — free 10 m vegetation phenology & productivity product (derived layer, not raw sensor)
- Planet Tanager / commercial spaceborne hyperspectral (new constellation)
- Airborne/UAV LiDAR and spaceborne structure for the 7 m trellis 3D (canopy height/volume) — the synthesis flags 'LiDAR absent in hop literature' but does not consider it as a buildable modality
- Commercial sub-meter X-band SAR (ICEYE, Capella) for all-weather structure/damage — only ESA C-band S1 is considered
- Upcoming high-res thermal missions (Copernicus LSTM, NASA SBG) and thermal-sharpening products — the field-scale-ET future the FAO-56 service would actually want
- Sentinel-3 SLSTR/OLCI and SMAP/ASCAT — coarse, but relevant for regional LST/soil-moisture context and worth an explicit 'too coarse' dismissal
- In-field / proximal ground sensors (soil-moisture probes, sap-flow, leaf-wetness, handheld/tractor spectrometers) — the cheap field-precise + ground-truth tier missing between satellite and UAV
- Sentinel-1 InSAR coherence (not just VV/VH backscatter) as a distinct canopy-structure/change channel

# Vegetation Indices & Biophysical Retrieval for Hops on a Vertical Trellis: What the Red-Edge Can (and Can't) Do

> Teil der Satelliten-Recherche (Deep-Research-Schwarm, 2026-06-28, Workflow `satellite-hops-research`).
> Facette: `indices`. Übersicht & Empfehlung: `README.md`. Geometrie-Vorabbefund: `field-scale-backtest.md`.

**Zusammenfassung:** For hops the strongest evidence base is a 4-year (2020-2023) UAV multispectral study in Czech hop gardens: NDVI, GNDVI, NDRE, CIR (CIre) and SAVI explained up to ~61% of yield variance (best in late season) but only ~18% of alpha-acid (quality) variance — vigor/biomass are trackable, brewing quality essentially is not, from indices alone. The red-edge is the single most important spectral region for hops: it resists NDVI's saturation above LAI~3 (typical of a full 7 m bine wall) and is the most direct proxy for canopy chlorophyll/nitrogen (NDRE/CIre reach r~0.89, R²~0.79 for foliar N in analogue crops). The central obstacle is geometry: a hop field is a set of ~7 m vertical leaf curtains over wide (~3 m) bare-soil inter-rows, so any nadir 10 m sensor integrates canopy + shadow + soil, and the signal swings with row orientation, sun angle and view/BRDF (well documented in trellised vineyards). Consequently Sentinel-2 (10 m VIS/NIR, 20 m red-edge) is honestly a regional-to-coarse-field-mean screening tool for hops — a 0.5 ha field yields only a handful of "pure" interior pixels — while genuinely field/sub-field-precise vigor, biomass and N/water maps require UAV multispectral (cm GSD) with reflectance calibration. SAVI/EVI2 (soil-adjusted) and SNAP/PROSAIL biophysical retrievals (LAI, fAPAR, canopy chlorophyll content) are the right tools to partially de-confound the soil/structure problem, but should be reported with explicit uncertainty.

**Feldskalen-Verdikt:** Mixed, and the split is sharp. SATELLITE (Sentinel-2, 10 m VIS/NIR, 20 m red-edge) is field-mean/regional SCREENING for hops, NOT field-precise: a 0.5 ha field (~70x70 m) yields only a handful of "pure" interior 10 m pixels and ~3-12 red-edge pixels, and the nadir view under-samples the 7 m vertical bine wall while mixing in wide bare inter-row soil/shadow — with signal swinging by row orientation, sun angle and BRDF. So Sentinel-2 can resolve a 0.5-2 ha field as a unit (trend, anomaly, between-field/season comparison) but cannot do teilflächengenaue within-field maps. UAV multispectral (cm GSD, RedEdge/Altum/Mavic 3M) IS genuinely field- and sub-field-precise: it can mask inter-row soil and produce per-row NDRE/CIre/SAVI and RTM LAI/CCC maps. Capability ceiling regardless of sensor: vigor/biomass/N are trackable (yield ~R²≤0.61 from the hop UAV study, best late-season), but brewing QUALITY (alpha-acid) is essentially not predictable from indices (~18% ceiling). Recommend branding satellite output explicitly as "regionales Screening / Feld-Check, nicht teilflächengenau" and reserving teilflächengenau claims for UAV.

## Bottom line for DoldenBlick

For a vertical 7 m hop trellis with wide rows, **vegetation indices reliably track vigor/biomass and (with red-edge) chlorophyll/N status, but only weakly predict yield and barely predict alpha-acid quality.** The honest split is: **UAV = field/sub-field precise; satellite (Sentinel-2) = regional or coarse field-mean screening.** Below, the hops-specific evidence first, then the analogues that fill the gaps, then the geometry problem that governs everything.

## The one strong hops-specific dataset (UAV)
A 4-year (2020-2023), conventional + organic, multi-cultivar UAV multispectral study of Czech hop gardens ("Assessment of UAV Imageries for Estimating Growth Vitality, Yield and Quality of Hop", *Remote Sensing* 17(6):970, 2025) is the most directly relevant work:
- **Indices used:** NDVI, GNDVI, **NDRE**, **CIR (= CIre, red-edge chlorophyll index)**, SAVI — as proxies for vitality/health/structure.
- **Yield:** spectral indices explained **up to ~61% of hop yield variance**, with **markedly better correlations in later growth stages** (full canopy) — consistent with red-edge indices working best once the bine wall is dense.
- **Quality (alpha-acid):** indices explained **a maximum of only ~18%** of alpha-acid content. This is the key cautionary finding: **alpha-acid is driven by genetics, cone development, heat/water late-season and harvest timing — not by canopy greenness.** Do not promise an "alpha potential" map from indices alone.
- A companion line of work (ISHS Acta Hort. 1328, "Evaluation of UAV and Sentinel-2 images to estimate condition of hop plants") computed NDVI from **both UAV and Sentinel-2**, using a binary green-vs-soil threshold to extract plant area; it confirmed NDVI separates conventional/organic gardens and cultivars, but flagged that the UAV-derived green area is **highly sensitive to flight parameters and crop (trellis) structure** — a direct warning about the mixed-pixel/geometry issue.

## What each index buys you (hops + trellised-row analogues)
- **NDVI** — robust early-season vigor/emergence and gap detection; **saturates above LAI ~3**, which a full hop wall exceeds, so it goes flat exactly when you most want late-season discrimination. Treat as a coarse "is the canopy closed / where are the gaps" layer.
- **NDRE (red-edge)** — the workhorse for hops mid/late season. Red-edge (~700-740 nm) **penetrates deeper into dense canopy and does not saturate**, and is far more sensitive to chlorophyll/N than NDVI. In analogue crops NDRE reaches **r ≈ 0.89 / R² ≈ 0.79 for foliar nitrogen**. This is why the hop study's yield signal strengthened late-season.
- **CIre / Clre (red-edge chlorophyll index)** — strongly correlated with **canopy chlorophyll content** in maize/soybean; more linear than NDRE at high chlorophyll, good for N status and senescence onset. The hop study's "CIR" is this family.
- **GNDVI** — green-band variant, similar chlorophyll sensitivity to NDRE where no red-edge band exists; useful as a cheap RGB-camera fallback.
- **SAVI / OSAVI / EVI2** — **soil-adjusted**; explicitly built to suppress the bare inter-row soil background that dominates wide-row hop/vineyard pixels. **EVI/EVI2** add an atmospheric/soil correction and resist saturation better than NDVI — preferred over raw NDVI for trellis canopies. These are the right "row-geometry-aware" indices.
- **Biophysical retrievals (not indices):** **LAI, fAPAR, fCover, canopy chlorophyll content (CCC), canopy water content** from RTM inversion (PROSAIL/PROSPECT) via the **Sentinel-2 SNAP Biophysical Processor** or Gaussian-process emulators (e.g. the GROUNDED-EO GPR LAI/fAPAR approach, *RSE* 2025). These are more physically meaningful than indices for vigor/biomass/N and partially absorb structural effects — but they are calibrated for closed/homogeneous canopies and carry **large uncertainty over discontinuous trellis rows**; report with confidence intervals.

## The red-edge specifically — why it matters most for hops
A mature hop field is **dense (LAI well above the NDVI saturation threshold) and tall**. Red light is fully absorbed at the canopy top, so NDVI "sees" only the upper leaf layer and flatlines. Red-edge photons are absorbed less, **penetrate further into the leaf curtain**, and respond to chlorophyll/N gradients through the canopy depth. Practically: **use red-edge indices (NDRE, CIre) for in-season N/vigor and senescence timing; use SAVI/EVI2 for early/sparse canopy; reserve NDVI for emergence and gap mapping.** On Sentinel-2 the three red-edge bands (B5 705 nm, B6 740 nm, B7 783 nm) are **20 m**, coarser than the 10 m VIS/NIR — a real limitation for small fields (see below).

## The geometry problem (the crux — be honest in the UI)
Hops are essentially **vertical leaf curtains ~7 m tall over ~3 m wide, often bare, inter-rows.** Every consequence below is documented for trellised/overhead vineyards, the closest published analogue (flag as analogue — vineyards are 2-3 m, not 7 m, so the *vertical* sampling problem is even worse for hops):
- **Mixed pixels:** Any 10 m pixel integrates canopy + shadow + inter-row soil/cover; vineyard studies state plainly that Sentinel-2 pixels are "necessarily mixed (vine/soil)." A nadir satellite mostly sees the canopy *tops* plus inter-row ground — it under-samples the vertical wall that holds most hop biomass.
- **Row orientation + sun-sensor geometry:** Sensor orientation relative to row direction has a **statistically significant effect on satellite-derived NDVI**; the same field reads differently depending on how much canopy wall vs. shaded inter-row is in view.
- **BRDF / view-illumination angle:** Off-nadir Sentinel-2 viewing and sun angle change apparent reflectance (documented degradation of retrieved crop parameters); for UAV, RedEdge-M sensors show measurable angular dependency on slopes. **BRDF normalization is mandatory**, not optional, for trellis time series.
- **Shadow:** Row shadow fraction varies with date/time/orientation and biases indices; row-orientation-specific shadow correction is an active research topic. Analogue row-crop work shows leaf-chlorophyll retrieval is **best from off-noon (~15:00) acquisitions for N-S rows** and benefits from canopy-structure minimization (LAI-insensitive indices, LICI) — relevant for choosing satellite overpass interpretation and any UAV flight windows.

## Field-scale resolution math (why satellite ≠ field-precise for hops)
- **Sentinel-2:** 10 m (VIS/NIR), 20 m (red-edge/SWIR); revisit ~5 days at the Hallertau latitude with S2A+S2B (now augmented by S2C). A **1 ha** square field ≈ 100×100 m ≈ ~100 pixels of 10 m, but a large fraction are edge pixels mixing with roads/neighbours; a **0.5 ha** field ≈ ~70×70 m yields only a handful of "pure" interior pixels — and at 20 m the red-edge gives only ~3-12 pixels. So Sentinel-2 supports **field-mean screening and between-field/between-season comparison, not teilflächengenaue (within-field) management.** This matches your standing premise that 10 m satellite on a 7 m trellis is regional screening.
- **UAV multispectral** (DJI Mavic 3M / MicaSense RedEdge / Altum, cm-to-dm GSD): genuinely **field- and sub-field-precise**, can mask out inter-row soil and isolate the canopy wall, and supports per-row vigor/N maps. Cost is operational (flights, reflectance-panel/DLS calibration, weather windows), not licensing.
- **Honest framing for users:** present satellite as a **"Feld-Check / regionales Screening"** (trend, anomaly flag, between-field comparison) and reserve **"teilflächengenau"** language for UAV — exactly the distinction already in CLAUDE.md.

## Practical recommendation for the Feld-Check
1. **Satellite (Sentinel-2, free, automatable):** field-mean **NDRE/CIre** (red-edge, 20 m) for in-season vigor/N trend + **NDVI/SAVI** (10 m) for emergence and gap/anomaly flags; always on a **pure-pixel interior mask** with **cloud masking and BRDF normalization**; report as a screening trend with a clear "regional, nicht teilflächengenau" caveat.
2. **Quality:** do **not** claim alpha-acid prediction from indices (~18% ceiling). At best, late-season red-edge trend + agromet (heat/water stress) as a weak qualitative indicator.
3. **UAV (optional premium tier):** field-precise NDRE/CIre/SAVI maps + RTM-derived LAI/CCC for within-field zones — this is where "teilflächengenau" is defensible.
